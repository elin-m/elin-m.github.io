Restaurant Guide

Usability and Information Architecture - UC San Diego - Winter 2013

Aim: To create a restaurant guide website as a gateway to more information for users.


User Needs

For three different user groups we interviewed three users each, asking about their restaurant habits, how they find out about new restaurants, and what they think about their currect methods of restaurant research. We observed them use similar websites. These interviews gave us a better understanding of how to design our restaurant guide to fit user needs.

User Scenarios

We analyzed interview answers and came up with generalizations for the whole group. Based on this, we decided on five different tasks that users from each group would like to accomplish with the help of our site. Then we explained how we would accommodate the user in regard to each specific scenario.

Designing the Site

We created a low fidelity wireframe to organize the layout of the site. We conducted a lot of research to find all the information that should go onto our site and selected the restaurants that were good enough for our users.

Deliverables

The aim of the website is to make the viewer hungry by the use of images that can communicate this brand. Photos of ethnic cuisines were chosen so that both culture and food could be seen, allowing the viewer to feel integrated into the situation and make them want to experience it for themselves.
