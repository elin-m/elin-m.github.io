Magazine Website

Practicum in Professional Web Design - UC San Diego - Spring 2013

Aim: To create a website for our clients, Fashion Quarterly Magazine, while continuing to meet with them throughout the design process.


Client & User Needs

We interviewed three people per user group from three different groups as identified by our clients. The interviews were conducted in a contextual manner by observing the user accessing the current site while asking them to explain what they were doing.


Competitive Analysis

We examined five college magazine websites to get a feel for common and well recognized site architecture and navigation terms. It also gave us a chance to explore good and bad ideas for the site.

Wireframes

We made high fidelity wireframes of the home page and low fidelity wireframes of all other pages, so that the client would not get hung up on colors and details. After feedback from our clients we re-designed the site accordingly.

Site Architecture

Plotting the site architecture helped us understand the flow of the site and get an overview of the scope of the project.

Storyboard

Plotting the site architecture helped us understand the flow of the site. We then walked our personas through their user scenarios by giving them a task and then showing the steps needed for the task to be accomplished.

Prototype

We built our site with HTML, CSS, and javascript/jQuery. Photoshop work was also involved. The focus throughout the site is mainly on the images, since this helps communicate the brand of a fashion magazine.
