Driverless Car for Families

Interaction Design Methodology - Chalmers University - Autumn 2014

Aim: To create an interactive car system for families using our knowledge of many different design methods.


Data Collection

We used many different methods to collect as much information as possible about family life, the role of the car in a family, and the future of the selfdriving car. These methods included ethnography, interviews, surveys, "A Day In The Life," bodystorming, and literature studies.



Analyzing Data

After discussing all the collected data, we organized the information into categories in an affinity diagram.


Personas

With the categorized data, we created persona families to represent our findings on family goals and priorities. The personas proved very helpful in identifying four main priorities in family life: social interaction, entertainment, organization of activities, and routine meshing.


Ideation

Our different ideation sessions were focused on the four themes we identified in the data through the personas. Methods such as 6-3-5 brainwriting, opposite brainstorming, and persona-based brainstorming were useful in coming up with many different possible solutions to the goals.


Idea Evaluation

Categorization and a ranking checklist helped to narrow down the options. With the ranking checklist, we each voted on whether an alternative fulfilled the different criteria we had decided on. We would later return to the ranking checklist to assess the validity of our ideas in relation to our data.


Prototype

After eventually settling on the idea of an augmented reality entertainment system for the car windows, we further developed the idea before creating paper prototypes and then a video. User testing of the paper prototypes gave us some insights on things to improve that we would have considered if we'd had more time.
